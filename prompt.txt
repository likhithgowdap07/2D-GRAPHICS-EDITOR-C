Q1. What is the purpose of HEIGHT and WIDTH?
A. They define the size of the canvas, which is 20 rows and 40 columns.

Q2. What is stored in canvas[HEIGHT][WIDTH]?
A. It stores the characters displayed on the screen. _ means empty space, and * means a drawn shape.

Q3. What does the Shape structure store?
A. It stores the shape’s ID, type, position, size values, and whether the shape is active.

Q4. What does initializeCanvas() do?
A. It fills the entire canvas with _ characters.

Q5. What does drawLine() do?
A. It draws a horizontal line on one row from startCol to endCol.

Q6. What does drawRectangle() do?
A. It draws only the border of a rectangle, not a filled one.

Q7. What does drawTriangle() do?
A. It draws a right triangle using * characters.

Q8. What does drawCircle() do?
A. It draws a filled circle by checking whether each point lies inside the radius.

Q9. Why is redrawCanvas() needed?
A. It clears the canvas and draws all active shapes again after adding, deleting, or modifying a shape.

Q10. What happens when you add a shape?
A. The program asks for the shape type and its values, stores it in the array, and redraws the canvas.

Q11. What happens when you delete a shape?
A. The program asks for the shape ID, marks it inactive, and redraws the canvas.

Q12. What happens when you modify a shape?
A. The program asks for the shape ID, updates its values, and redraws the canvas.

Q13. What does listShapes() show?
A. It prints the IDs and types of all active shapes.

Q14. What does option 6 do in the menu?
A. It clears the canvas and removes all stored shapes by setting shapeCount = 0.

Q15. What does option 7 do?
A. It exits the program.
